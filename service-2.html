Guidelines

A guideline is a statement by which to determine a course of action. A guideline aims to streamline particular processes according to a set routine or sound practice. Hence, in some circumstances, the purpose guidelines aim to serve is to make adaptable recommendations.

Visitor's Guidelines

We are committed to providing a safe, quiet and healthy environment for our patients, their family and friends. We recognise the therapeutic benefit and reassurance visitors provide our patients which accelerate their healing process. Hence request visitor’s to abide by the following guidelines:

  • People with fever, cold sores, cough, nasal drainage, colds, diarrhea, or any contagious disease should not visit patients.
  • Visitors should maintain a quiet environment and avoid unnecessary noise to optimize patient rest. Keep visits short and quiet.
  • Due to confidentiality, visitors may be asked to leave the room during tests or treatments or when doctor/nurses consult with a patient.
  • Visitors must clean their hands when entering and leaving the clinical ward/unit. Alcohol gel or liquid soap and water can be used.
  • For safety reasons, visitor’s are not permitted in isolation areas.
  • Do not bring eatables, battery operated toys, cameras and children (below 12 years of age) in the hospital.
  • Children cannot be left unattended in the entrance lobby or parking lot. Any child leaving the visiting area for any reason must be accompanied by an adult.
  • Visitors are required to screen all purses, handbags, or other carrying items with the Security personnel.
  • Pet animals are not permitted.
  • The entire hospital premise is a “NO SMOKING ZONE”; please do not smoke inside the hospital campus.
  • In case of fire, use Fire staircase following signage.
  • Please avoid bringing costly items to the hospital. There might be a chance of losing/misplacing them.


Denial or Termination of a Visit

The following is a list of rules for visitors. Violation of these rules may result in the termination, denial of the visit, or suspension of visitation privileges for the visitor:

  • Visitors, who appear under the influence of alcohol or drugs (except medically prescribed drugs), display disorderly or inappropriate behavior, or refuse to follow staff directives, may be denied visitation.
  • Visitors who refuse or fail to produce visitor passes issued by the hospital.
  • Narcotic or illegal drugs, alcohol and weapons are not permitted inside the hospital premises. Persons found to be attempting to bring any of these items in the Hospital may be subject to Legal prosecution.
  • Visitor refuses to submit to search procedures.
